# Salt Baked Potatoes

The perfect, light, fluffy, tender, moist potatoes with crispy skin, and delicious flavor. This method of salt baking the potatoes results in perfect potatoes every time.

- **Servings:** {4}
- [Source](https://www.eazypeazymealz.com/salt-baked-potatoes/)
- **Prep Time:** 10 minutes
- **Cook Time:** 1 hour 45 minutes
- **Total Time:** 1 hour 55 minutes

## Ingredients

- {2 1/2} cups Kosher salt
- {4} russet potatoes, medium sized, scrubbed and dried
- {2} sprigs fresh rosemary, minced
- {1} whole head garlic, garlic cloves removed from papery part
- {4} teaspoons canola oil
- {4} tablespoons unsalted butter, softened (optional, only if you want to make garlic butter for serving)

## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and heat oven to 450°F.
1. Select and wash your potatoes, removing any dirt. Then dry off.
1. Spread salt in even layer in 9 by 13-inch baking dish. Stir rosemary into the salt.
1. Nestle potatoes into the salt, leaving plenty of space between potatoes so they are not touching.
1. Add the head of garlic, cut side up, to baking dish.
1. Cover baking dish with foil or a lid, covering tightly.
1. Bake 1 hour 15 minutes; about 30 minutes in, remove garlic from pan so it doesn't burn. Then after the baking time, remove the pan from oven and remove the foil or lid.
1. Increase oven temperature to 500°F.
1. Brush the potatoes with canola oil (1 tsp for each potato) and return to the baking dish, and the baking dish to the oven.
1. Bake another 15-20 minutes until potatoes are tender.
1. While potatoes finish baking, add garlic to melted butter to serve with potatoes, whisking it with a fork to evenly distribute garlic through the butter.
1. When potatoes are finished baking, brush any excess salt from the skin of the potatoes (carefully, they will be hot).
1. Use a fork to pierce potatoes in a cross shape, and squeeze potatoes to split them open to reveal the fluffy interior.
1. Serve immediately with the garlic butter or toppings of your choice.

## Notes

- **Salt:** Kosher or table salt is best for this recipe.
- **Reuse:** The salt can be reused for this recipe again, or used for other things like brines.
- **Optional herbs:** The herbs and garlic are optional; these potatoes can be prepared with just the salt and oil, then topped with other garnishes such as sour cream, chives, crumbled bacon, and/or shredded cheese for a different flavor profile.
