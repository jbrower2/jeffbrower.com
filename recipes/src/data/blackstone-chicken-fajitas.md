# Easy Blackstone Chicken Fajitas

Looking for a delicious and easy way to make fajitas? Look no further than your Blackstone Griddle! With just a few simple ingredients, you can have a fantastic meal that the whole family will love. So fire up your griddle and get cooking!

- **Servings:** {8}
- [Source](https://wendypolisi.com/blackstone-fajitas/)
- **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
- **Cook Time:** 10 minutes
- **Marinating Time:** 2 hours
- **Total Time:** 2 hours 25 minutes

## Ingredients

### Chicken

- {1/2} cup avocado oil or your favorite high heat oil
- {2} tablespoons lime juice
- {2} teaspoons chile powder
- {2} teaspoons sea salt
- {1} teaspoon garlic powder
- {1} teaspoon ground cumin
- {1/4} teaspoon cayenne pepper
- {2 1/2} pounds boneless skinless chicken, cut into 1/4 inch strips

### Fajitas

- oil for the griddle
- {2} yellow onions, sliced
- {2} bell peppers, cut into strips
- {8} tortillas, corn or fajita-sized flour
- for serving: shredded cheddar cheese, sour cream, lime wedges, tomatoes, cilantro or salsa

## Instructions

1. Combine the avocado oil, lime juice, chili powder, salt, garlic powder, cumin, and cayenne pepper in a mason jar or blender.
1. Shake or blend until well mixed..
1. Place the chicken in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over it. Allow to marinate in the refrigerator for 2 hours.
1. Remove the chicken from the marinade and discard.
1. Heat your Blackstone griddle to medium-high heat.
1. Brush with avocado oil.
1. Add the onions and peppers to one side of the griddle and the chicken to the other.
1. Cook for about 6 to 9 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are lightly charred.
1. Remove from the heat.
1. Heat the tortillas until warmed through, about 1 minute.
1. Serve the meat and veggies with the warm tortillas and desired toppings.

## Notes

- **Storage:** Leftovers may be stored in a sealed container in the refrigerator for three days.
