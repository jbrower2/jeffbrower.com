# Banana Cream Pie

- **Servings:** {16}
- **Yield:** {2} pies

## Ingredients

- {2} Pie Crust, baked
- {8} cup Milk
- {1/4} tsp Salt
- {1} Vanilla Bean, split lengthwise
- {24} Egg Yolk
- {2 2/3} cup Sugar
- {1} cup Cornstarch
- {6} tbsp Butter, cold/in pieces
- {6} Banana, sliced

## Instructions

1. Combine the milk, salt, and vanilla bean in a non-aluminum pan and heat, stirring occasionally
1. As soon as the mixture begins to boil, turn off the heat and stir the mixture for 1 minute
1. Whisk the egg yolks and sugar together until thick and pale yellow, then gradually whisk in the cornstarch
1. Gradually whisk in half of the hot milk mixture
1. Pour the yolk-milk mixture back into the saucepan with the milk mixture and cook, whisking frequently, until the mixture boils and begins to thicken
1. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook, stirring, about 5 minutes more, until the mixture no longer tastes starchy
1. The mixture should become very thick, like a set custard
1. When cooked, turn off the heat
1. Stir in the butter until it melts completely and fold in the bananas
1. Pour the filling into the pre-baked pie shells
1. Push the banana slices below the surface to prevent them from browning, then smooth the tops
1. Cover with plastic wrap, gently pressing the plastic wrap against the surface to prevent a skin from forming
1. Refrigerate at least 7 hours or overnight, and serve with whipped cream
