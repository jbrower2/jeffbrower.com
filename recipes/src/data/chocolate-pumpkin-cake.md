# Chocolate Pumpkin Cake

- **Servings:** {12}
- **Yield:** {1} 2-layer cake

## Ingredients

- {1 3/4} cup All-purpose Flour
- {3/4} cup Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
- {2} tsp Baking Powder
- {1} tsp Baking Soda
- {1/2} tbsp Cinnamon
- {1/2} tsp Nutmeg
- {1/2} cup Buttermilk
- {1} cup Pumpkin Puree
- {1} tsp Vanilla Extract
- {1 1/2} stick Butter, softened
- {1} cup Brown Sugar
- {1} cup Sugar
- {3} Egg
- Frosting

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350 degrees F
1. Lightly butter three 8-inch cake pans and fit each bottom with an 8-inch circle of parchment paper
1. Lightly butter the parchment paper
1. Set aside
1. Sift the flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a large bowl and set aside
1. Combine the buttermilk, pumpkin, and vanilla in a medium bowl and set aside
1. Beat the butter and sugars together in a large bowl, with an electric mixer set on medium speed, until fluffy
1. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition, until the mixture is smooth and light
1. Alternately add the flour mixture and buttermilk mixture, blending well after each addition
1. Divide the batter among the pans and bake about 35 minutes until a wooden skewer inserted into the middle comes out clean
1. Cool the cakes in the pan for 20 minutes
1. Remove cakes and cool
1. Trim each of the layers
1. Place one layer on a cake plate and top with one third of the frosting
1. Repeat with the second and third layers
1. To ensure that the cake layers do not shift, cut three skewers to 1/4 inch shorter than the full height of the cake and insert them before icing the top layer
1. Refrigerate until ready to serve
