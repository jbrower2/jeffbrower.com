# Churros

- **Servings:** {12}
- **Yield:** {12} churros

## Ingredients

- {1} cup Water
- {1 1/2} tbsp Sugar
- {1/16} tsp Salt
- {1} cup Self-rising Flour
- {2} Egg
- {1} Egg Yolk
- {0} Peanut Oil
- {6} oz Chocolates, chopped

## Instructions

1. Make the dough by heating water, sugar and salt in saucepan over medium heat until hot
1. Add the flour all at once and stir vigorously for 1 minute on the heat to dry the mixture out
1. Remove from heat and transfer the dough to a stand mixer bowl; mix on low speed until there is no longer steam escaping from the dough
1. With the mixer running on medium, add the eggs, 1 at a time, incorporating completely until a shiny, smooth texture forms before adding the next egg, and then the egg yolk
1. Scrape down the mixer bowl with a rubber spatula between each addition
1. Transfer the dough to the piping bag
1. Pour oil into a large heavy bottomed pot to a depth of 4 inches; heat over medium-high heat to 375 degrees F
1. To fry the churros, carefully squeeze the dough from the piping bag directly over the oil in 3 to 4-inch lengths, cutting the dough at the tip with a butter knife and gently dropping them into the oil
1. Turn frequently with a Chinese strainer or tongs until golden brown, about 3 minutes
1. Work in batches to prevent overcrowding the pan and allowing the oil to reheat to 375 degrees F between each batch
1. Drain the churros on a paper towels and then toss in sugar to coat while still warm
1. Melt the chocolate in a glass bowl set over a pan of barely simmering water
1. Arrange the curros on a serving platter and serve the chocolate sauce alongside
1. Enjoy!
