# Peanut Butter-Chocolate Chip-Bacon Cookies

- **Servings:** {12}
- **Yield:** {12} cookies

## Ingredients

- {1 1/4} cup All-purpose Flour
- {1/4} tsp Baking Soda
- {1/4} tsp Baking Powder
- {1/8} tsp Cinnamon
- {1/16} tsp Chipotle Powder
- {1/4} tsp Salt
- {1/2} lb Bacon
- {1/2} stick Butter
- {1/2} cup Peanut Butter
- {1/2} cup Sugar
- {1/2} cup Brown Sugar
- {1} Egg
- {1} tsp Vanilla Extract
- {1/2} cup Peanuts, honey-roasted and chopped
- {1/3} cup Chocolate Chips

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F
1. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper
1. Combine the flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, chile powder and salt in a large bowl
1. Cook the bacon in a large skillet over medium heat until crisp, about 4 to 6 minutes per side
1. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate; reserve 2 tablespoons of the drippings and set aside to cool
1. Crumble the bacon, discarding any chewy bits
1. Beat the butter and reserved bacon drippings in a large bowl with a mixer on medium-high speed until smooth, about 1 minute
1. Beat in the peanut butter until combined, about 1 minute
1. Beat in the granulated and light brown sugar until creamy, about 4 minutes, then add the egg and vanilla and beat until light and fluffy, about 2 more minutes
1. Reduce the mixer speed to low; add the flour mixture in 2 additions, scraping down the bowl as needed, until just combined
1. Stir in the peanuts and all but 2 tablespoons each of the chocolate chips and bacon
1. Form the dough into 12 balls and arrange 2 inches apart on the prepared baking sheets
1. Flatten with your fingers (the cookies will not spread in the oven); press the reserved bacon and chocolate chips on top
1. Bake until golden, 12 to 14 minutes
1. Let the cookies cool 2 minutes on the baking sheets, then transfer to a rack to cool completely
1. Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days
