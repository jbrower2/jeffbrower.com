# Chocolate Chip Cupcakes

- **Servings:** {24}
- **Yield:** {24} cupcakes

## Ingredients

- {3} stick Butter
- {1 1/2} cup Brown Sugar
- {4} Egg
- {2 2/3} cup All-purpose Flour
- {1} tsp Baking Powder
- {1} tsp Baking Soda
- {1/4} tsp Salt
- {1} cup Milk
- {2} tsp Vanilla Extract
- {1} cup Chocolate Chips
- {1/2} stick Butter
- {6} tbsp Brown Sugar
- {1 1/8} cup All-purpose Flour
- {7} oz Condensed Milk
- {1/2} tsp Vanilla Extract
- {1/4} cup Mini Chocolate Chips

## Instructions

1. To make the cupcakes, preheat the oven to 350° F
1. Line two cupcake pans with paper liners (24 total)
1. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, combine the butter and brown sugar
1. Beat together on medium-high speed until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes
1. Mix in the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition and scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed
1. Combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl
1. Stir together to blend
1. Add the dry ingredients to the mixer bowl on low speed, alternating with the milk, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients, mixing each addition just until incorporated
1. Blend in the vanilla
1. Fold in the chocolate chips with a spatula
1. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cupcake liners
1. Bake for 18-20 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean
1. Allow to cool in the pan 5-10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely
1. To make the cookie dough filling (last 6 ingredients), combine the butter and sugar in a mixing bowl and cream on medium-high speed until light and fluffy, about 2 minutes
1. Beat in the flour, Condensed Milk and vanilla until incorporated and smooth
1. Stir in the chocolate chips
1. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until the mixture has firmed up a bit, about an hour
1. To fill the cupcakes, cut a cone-shaped portion out of the center of each cupcake
1. Fill each hole with a chunk of the chilled cookie dough mixture
