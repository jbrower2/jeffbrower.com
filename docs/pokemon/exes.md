Cards are grouped by the energy their evolution line actually needs to attack (not the Pokémon's on-card type). A line that needs 2–3 different energies lives under Multi; a line whose ex attacks purely with Colorless lives under Colorless. Where an ex is mono-energy but its pre-evolutions/siblings use a second type, that's noted in parentheses.

Multi:

- Alolan Exeggutor ex #0102 (grass + water + fighting) - can knock out opponent basic pokemon, and makes it easy to attach energy
- Galvantula ex #0595 (lightning + grass + fighting) - pretty heavy hitter, and can prevent your opponent from playing all cards from their hand
- Cinderace ex #0813 (fire + fighting + dark) - big damage every other turn, and can do medium damage to benched pokemon
- Salamence ex #0371 (fire + water) - Dragon Impact hits for 300, or Wide Blast spreads 50 to every benched pokemon using only fire
- Tatsugiri ex #0978 (fire + water + dark) - solid 100 hitter that can also dig for pokemon and load your bench
- Flygon ex #0328 (fighting + water + metal, or just fighting) - Reversing Storm is a mono-fighting hit-and-run; the fun payoff Sonic Peridot does 100 to every opposing ex/V
- Palossand ex #0769 (fighting + psychic + water, or just colorless) - Sand Tomb is a plain 160 colorless hit; Barite Jail is a wild control move that drops every benched pokemon to 100 HP
- Mega Latias ex #0380 (fire + psychic) - one big 300 nuke, but discards all its energy
- Miraidon ex #1008 (lightning + psychic) - fast, cheap two-energy attacker, hits harder into damaged pokemon
- Mega Dragalge ex #0690 (water + dark) - strips all tools and special energy off your opponent, then poisons for a brutal 160/turn

Notes:

- "≤ $1 ex" means the cheapest legal printing is at or under $1.00 - it's your one premium slot. Prices drift, so double-check before you commit.
- Cheapest legal ex in the set: Mega Zygarde ex ($0.40) and Salazzle ex ($0.48) are the only ex cards that fit a ≤$0.50 support slot, so you could even run one as a secondary attacker.
- Dragon-type pokemon don't have their own energy, so they're filed under whatever energy they actually pay for (e.g. Salamence under Multi, Noivern under Colorless).
